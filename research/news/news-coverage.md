# Free Geek Portland - News Coverage Research

Compiled: December 13, 2025

## Major News Stories

### 1. 25th Anniversary Coverage (April 2025)
**Source:** [KGW](https://www.kgw.com/article/news/local/good-energy/free-geek-celebrates-25-years-earth-day/283-3949dc15-0059-43a3-ab4a-5365d41b7c24)

Free Geek celebrated 25 years on Earth Day 2025. Key highlights:
- Started as a small Earth Day recycling pop-up at Pioneer Courthouse Square
- Has diverted 82 million pounds of electronic waste from landfills since founding
- Considered a pioneer in Oregon's e-waste and digital access space
- In 2023 alone, refurbished nearly 10,000 computers and almost 700 mobile devices

### 2. Fire Incident and Recovery (September 2024)
**Sources:**
- [KGW](https://www.kgw.com/article/news/local/free-geek-closed-fire-building-damaged/283-c8fff90d-84a7-472f-811e-688c83f93625)
- [KOIN](https://www.koin.com/news/portland/free-geek-temporarily-closes-after-fire-damages-donation-center/)
- [Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/e-scrap/2024/09/30/fire-at-nonprofit-free-geek-prompts-community-support/)

**Incident Details:**
- September 14, 2024, ~2 a.m.: Fire started outside receiving and recycling area
- Location: Near SE 10th Avenue and SE Market Street
- Damage: Donation center and dumpsters damaged, building interior unaffected
- No injuries reported
- 12 team members temporarily out of work

**Community Response:**
- Fundraising goal: $25,000
- 239 donors contributed over $23,800
- Organization stated: "The outpour of support from our community has been overwhelming"
- Reopened for limited parking lot drop-offs after more than a week

### 3. $2 Million Grant (December 2023)
**Source:** [Willamette Week](https://www.wweek.com/promotions/2023/12/11/nonprofit-free-geek-gets-2-million-grant-to-help-multnomah-county-residents-get-digitally-connected/)

**Grant Details:**
- Amount: $2 million
- Funders: Comcast and Mt. Hood Regulatory Commission (MHCRC)
- Purpose: Support Free Geek's Digital Equity Action Plan

**Expansion Plans:**
- Expand services to East Portland, Gresham, Fairview, Wood Village, and Troutdale
- Partner with community-based organizations
- Provide technology education, digital tools, computers, and internet access
- Help partner organizations develop their own digital equity programs
- Implementation began early 2024

**Target Statistics:**
- ~60,000 households in five east Multnomah County neighborhoods
- ~30,000 qualify for federal broadband discounts but haven't enrolled
- ~3,300 lack functional computers

### 4. COVID-19 Pandemic Response (June 2020)
**Source:** [OPB](https://www.opb.org/news/article/low-cost-laptop-computer-portland-free-geek-coronavirus/)

**Demand Surge:**
- Laptop requests spiked nearly 4,000%
- From ~300 monthly requests to 3,000 per week

**Key Quote - Hilary Shohoney, Director of Community Development:**
> "Folks need the internet to be able to see a doctor, to pay their bills, do work so they can continue to get a paycheck or get an education. It touches every area of our life right now."

**Request Sources:**
- Students needing online K-12 and college access
- Adult children seeking help for aging parents unable to visit doctors
- People requiring mental health and addiction treatment services

**Context:**
- ~17% of Portland residents lacked internet access
- Nearly 40% lacked access in rural Northwest areas
